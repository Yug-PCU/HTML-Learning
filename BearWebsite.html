Overview

The brown bear (Ursus arctos) is a large bear species native to northern Eurasia and North America. Among the largest living terrestrial carnivores, it is presently listed as Least Concern by the IUCN.

Subspecies

Arctos
Ursus arctos arctos
›
The Eurasian brown bear — the nominate subspecies, found across a broad range from Western Europe through Russia.
Horribilis
Ursus arctos horribilis
›
The North American grizzly bear — known for its pronounced shoulder hump and formidable size in interior regions of Alaska and Canada.
Nelsoni
Ursus arctos nelsoni
›
The Mexican grizzly bear — now extinct. Last confirmed sighting in 1964. Historically inhabited the Sierra Madre highlands of northern Mexico.
